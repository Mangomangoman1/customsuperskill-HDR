Emergency guide

Something went wrong. Here's what to do.

Immediate first-aid for common device emergencies. These steps can save your data and sometimes prevent further damage before you get to us.

Find your problem

Symptom decoder

What happened to your device?

Dropped in water

Phone, laptop, or tablet got wet. Power it off immediately. Do not charge it. Do not put it in rice.

Act now

Screen is cracked

Glass is shattered or display has lines. If glass is loose, cover it with tape to prevent cuts. Back up data if screen still works.

Same-day fix

Will not turn on

Black screen, no response to charging. Try a different cable and wall outlet first. Hold power button 30 seconds.

Bring it in

Overheating badly

Device is hot to touch, may smell burnt. Power it off immediately. Do not put it in a freezer.

Act now

Liquid spilled on keyboard

Turn it upside down immediately. Unplug charger. Do not shake it aggressively. Leave it off.

Act now

Stuck in boot loop

Device keeps restarting, never reaches home screen. Try forcing a hard reset. If that fails, it needs diagnosis.

Bring it in

Water damage protocol

The first 60 minutes matter

Minute 0: Power off

Do not try to turn it on to check if it works. Every second powered on increases corrosion risk.

Minute 1: Remove cases and SIM

Take off any case, remove the SIM tray, and gently shake out visible water. Do not use a hair dryer.

Minute 5: Pat dry externally

Use a lint-free cloth. Do not stick anything into charging ports or speaker grills.

Minute 10: Do not use rice

Rice does not absorb internal moisture and can introduce starch dust. Leave it in open air, screen-side down.

Hour 1: Bring it in

The sooner we open it and clean the logic board, the better the odds. Corrosion starts within hours, not days.

Emergency contact

If your device is actively smoking, sparking, or swelling, do not attempt to power it off with your hands. Move it to a non-flammable surface and call us.

Data safety

Can your data be saved?

In most cases, yes. Here is what determines whether we can recover your photos, documents, and files.

Usually recoverable

Cracked screen, dead battery, water damage cleaned quickly, failed software update. The storage chip is usually intact.

Maybe recoverable

Severe water damage, logic board failure, multiple drop impacts. We attempt chip-off recovery. Success rate around 60-70%.

Usually not recoverable

Crushed storage chip, fire damage, encryption with forgotten password. We will tell you honestly before attempting anything.

When to bring it in vs. when to wait

Not every problem needs immediate repair. Here is how to tell.

Come in today

  • — Water damage (any amount)
  • — Overheating or burning smell
  • — Swollen battery
  • — Smoke or sparks
  • — Device bent or frame cracked

Can wait a day or two

  • — Cracked screen (still works)
  • — Battery draining fast
  • — Speaker quiet or distorted
  • — Charging port loose
  • — Camera blurry
